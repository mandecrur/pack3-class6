Casuísticas de práctica
11
Students have practised ordering food in English using the expression "I would like a sandwich, please" during several lessons. Now, their teacher sets up a simulated fast-food restaurant in the classroom and students must use those same expressions to place their orders. What type of transfer does this activity promote?
A
Far transfer, because the simulated restaurant is a completely different context from the one used during practice.
B
Near transfer, because the context (ordering food) is similar to the one in which the language was originally practised.
C
Metacognitive transfer, because students reflect on how they use the language in a new situation.
Perkins y Salomon (1992) distinguen entre transferencia cercana (near transfer: aplicación en un contexto similar al de aprendizaje) y transferencia lejana (far transfer: aplicación en un contexto bastante diferente). El role-play del restaurante dentro del aula sigue siendo un contexto muy similar al de práctica original; por tanto, corresponde a transferencia cercana. La "metacognitive transfer" no es una categoría reconocida en la literatura de referencia.
12
Carmen's students learned how to introduce themselves in English: "Hi, my name is... I'm from... I like..." All of them perform perfectly in the written exercises. However, when a foreign volunteer visits the school, none of them manages to introduce themselves spontaneously. Which problem does this situation reveal?
A
A failure in sustained attention, because students did not focus during the lessons.
B
Absence of functional transfer, because the knowledge remained tied to the written practice context and was not mobilised toward a real communicative situation.
C
A retrieval failure, because students cannot recall the expressions stored in their long-term memory.
Perkins y Salomon (1992) señalan que la transferencia lejana es menos frecuente y requiere enseñanza deliberada. Los alumnos dominan los ejercicios escritos, lo que descarta problemas de atención o recuperación. El problema es que el aprendizaje no se movilizó hacia la comunicación auténtica con un hablante real: no hubo transferencia funcional. Esta es la señal de alerta más importante para el docente de inglés.
13
Read Marco's teaching sequence for a dialogue unit on professions:

  • Session 1: He displays a complete model dialogue on the board for students to read and repeat.
  • Session 2: He provides a gapped outline for students to complete with their own information.
  • Session 3: He asks students to create their own original dialogue with no support at all.

Which pedagogical strategy does this sequence describe?
A
Metacognitive evaluation, because students assess their own performance at the end of each session.
B
Far transfer, because students apply the dialogue to increasingly different contexts.
C
Scaffolding, because Marco provides regulated and decreasing support until students achieve full autonomy.
El scaffolding (Wood, Bruner y Ross, 1976) es el soporte temporal y decreciente que guía al estudiante a través de su Zona de Desarrollo Próximo (Vygotsky, 1978) hasta alcanzar la autonomía. La secuencia de Marco sigue exactamente ese patrón: máximo apoyo → apoyo parcial → producción libre. No es transferencia porque los contextos no cambian sustancialmente; no es evaluación metacognitiva porque no se pide reflexión sobre el proceso.
14
Throughout the entire school year, Beatriz always gives her students a sentence frame to produce any oral or written task. She never withdraws this support. What pedagogical problem does Beatriz's practice generate?
A
It activates the alerting network of the attentional system, keeping students dependent on visual stimuli.
B
It generates permanent dependence on the teacher, preventing students from reaching the autonomy that is the ultimate goal of scaffolding.
C
It promotes extrinsic motivation, since students only produce language when a frame is provided.
El andamiaje debe ser temporal y retirarse progresivamente (Wood, Bruner y Ross, 1976). Si el soporte nunca se retira, el alumno no transita por su ZPD hacia la autonomía: se genera dependencia permanente en el docente. Este es uno de los dos errores clásicos del scaffolding: el otro es retirarlo demasiado pronto. Ninguna de las otras opciones describe correctamente las consecuencias pedagógicas de esta práctica.
15
Rosa is teaching her beginner students to write emails in English. From the very first session, she asks them to write a complete formal email independently, with no model or language support. Most students are unable to complete the task and become frustrated. What mistake has Rosa made?
A
She activated far transfer too early, before students had consolidated the language in a familiar context.
B
She withdrew the scaffolding too soon, asking for full autonomous production before students had mastered the basic structures.
C
She failed to promote intrinsic motivation, since the email task had no authentic communicative purpose for the students.
El error opuesto al de nunca retirar el andamiaje es retirarlo demasiado pronto. Rosa exige producción libre desde la primera sesión, sin haber provisto un modelo completo ni apoyo parcial previo. Esto supera la Zona de Desarrollo Próximo de los alumnos y genera frustración. El scaffolding correcto sigue la secuencia: modelo completo → apoyo parcial → autonomía. No es un problema de transferencia ni de motivación.
